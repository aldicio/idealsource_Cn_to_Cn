The stable types in the source of corank $1$ map germ in ${\mathcal O}(n,n)$

Introduction

The multiple point spaces of a map germ from $({\mathbb C}^n, 0)$ to $({\mathbb C}^p, 0)$ play an important role in the study of its geometry, as well as the topology of the discriminant of a stable perturbation. The description of zero-stable types in the source and in the target for corank $1$ map germs from $({\mathbb C}^n,0)$ to $({\mathbb C}^n,0)$, is shown by Marar, Montaldi, Ruas, [1]. And the generalization for this description for all $r$-stable types, with $0 \leq r \leq n-1$, is shown by Jorge-Pérez, Levcovitz, Saia, [2]. Below a Singular library to computing the definition ideal of these $r$-stable types of $f$.

Let $f\in {\mathcal O}(n,n)$ be a finitely determined map germ of corank $1$. Choosing linearly adapted coordinates, we can write $f(x,z)=(x_1, \dots, x_{n-1},g(x,z))$, where $x =(x_1, \dots, x_{n-1}) \in {\mathbb C}^{n-1}$, $z\in {\mathbb C}$ and $g: ({\mathbb C}^n,0) \to ({\mathbb C},0)$ is a polynomial. For each partition ${\mathcal P}=(r_1,\cdots,r_{\ell})$ of an integer $m\leq n$, i.e, $r_1+\cdots +r_{\ell}=m$, we consider the subset $D^{\ell}(f,{\mathcal P})$ of ${\mathbb C}^{n-1}\times {\mathbb C}^{\ell}$, with $\ell :={\rm length}({\mathcal P})$, defined by $$D^{\ell}(f,{\mathcal P})\!=\!closure\!\left\{\begin{array}{l} \!(x,z_1,\cdots,z_{\ell})\!\in \! {\mathbb C}^{n-1}\!\times\! {\mathbb C}^{\ell} \colon \end{array} \begin{array}{l} z_i\neq z_j,\\ f(x,z_i)=f(x,z_j) \ {\rm and}\\ f\,\, \mbox{ has a singularity of type}\\ A_{r_j} \ \mbox{at} \ (x,z_j) \end{array}\right\}$$ where `closure' means the analytic closure in ${\mathbb C}^{n-1}\times {\mathbb C}^{\ell}$.

We remark that when $m=n$, the subsets $D^n(f,{\mathcal P})$ are called zero-schemes and are related to the $0$-stable types

Example: a) For the partition ${\mathcal P}=(1,\cdots,1)$ of $m$ with $1+\cdots+1=m=\ell$, we have $D^{\ell}(f,{\mathcal P})=D^{\ell}(f)$, where $D^{\ell}(f)$ denotes the set of ordinary $\ell$-multiple points of $f$.

b) For the partition ${\mathcal P}=(r_i)$, then $r_i=m$, $\ell =1$, and $D^{1}(f,{\mathcal P})=\Sigma^{1,\cdots,1}(f),$ where $\Sigma^{1,\cdots,1}(f)$ is the set of singularities of type $\Sigma^{1, \ldots ,1}$ of $f$ with $ 1,\cdots,1$ repeated ${r_i}$-times, i.e, $f$ has a singularity of type $A_{r_i}$.

c) For the partition ${\mathcal P}=(r_1,\cdots,r_{\ell})$ of $m\leq n$, $D^{\ell}(f,{\mathcal P})$ is related to the $A_{r_1,\cdots,r_{\ell}}$ multigerms.

Steps to use the Singular library (idealsource.lib) to obtain the definition ideal of these $r$-stable types $D^{\ell}(f,{\mathcal P})$ of $f$.

In SINGULAR environment:
1. LIB "idealsource.lib'';
"idealsource" works with a ring of parameters, but with unfolding can be faster.

If $f(x,y)=(x,y^4+xy)$ and $f_u(x,y)=(x,y^4+xy+uy^2)$ and partition $=$ [1,1], use:

2. > ring r=(0,u),(x,y),dp; //With parameters
> idealsource([x,y4+xy+uy2],[1,1]);
> ring r=0,(u,x,y),ds; //As unfolding
> idealsource([u,x,y4+xy+uy2],[1,1]);"
